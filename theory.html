Social Cognitive Theory (SIT) describes that humans do not just react to the environment, they actively control how they interact with the environment (Bandura, 2001). This theory suggests that our target audience could choose not to be a bystander and instead take control of their security and actively consider purchasing the Ring to feel safer in their home. SIT is also a great example of how humans learn from observed modeled behavior (Bandura, 2001; Schunk, D. H., 2012; Knobloch-Westerwick & Sarge, 2015). Bandura (2001) states that “…models serve as transmitters of knowledge, competencies, values, cognitive skills, and new styles of behavior” (p.2).

SIT has a four-step process that consists of Attention, Retention, Production, and Motivation. All four will be present on our website by ensuring that our modeled behavior is visible and that the product details fit with the audience’s existing cognitive structures so they are relatable. 


 

 

In our persuasion effort, we will offer anecdotes of consumers who have had positive experiences with the Ring. The idea is that when you see someone utilize a product and then observe how it benefited them, the logical outcome is that the individual will think that that product, in this case the Ring, will equally or similarly help them.  



 In addition to personal anecdotes, reading tech expert reviews with positive takes on the Ring will also work as an impactful testimonial to weigh the benefits and costs of taking action to purchase the Ring. Hayward (2019) gave his final verdict by saying that the Ring is "Worth the expense." Adding that, “…the Ring Video Doorbell Pro is an excellent smart doorbell camera option. It's a feature-rich device that works very well in everyday usage, tracking movement and letting you jump into remote two-way conversations with anyone who presses the doorbell buzzer.”
